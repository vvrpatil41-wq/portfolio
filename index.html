About Me

I am a Master's student in Vehicle Technology at the University of Stuttgart with a strong background in CAD design and simulation. My expertise spans from creating complex 3D models in Siemens NX and CATIA V5 to performing advanced metal forming simulations in DEFORM and QForm.

At the Institute for Metal Forming Technology, I've contributed to cutting-edge research projects involving hybrid metal-plastic forming and optimization of cold forming processes. My work with Mercedes Benz on luxury vehicle concepts and involvement in Formula Student racing has deepened my passion for innovative automotive design.

Hybrid Metal-Plastic Forming

Research project on hybrid massive forming of metal-plastic composites. Created CAD models of gears using CATIA V5, performed digital measurements, and compared material densities between CAD and manufactured models using 3D scans and GOM Inspect.

CATIA V5 GOM Inspect 3D Scanning Material Analysis

Metal Forming Simulation & Optimization

Conducted comprehensive metal forming simulations using DEFORM and QForm. Performed sensitivity, triaxiality, and effective strain analysis. Optimized tribological tests for various lubricants in cold forming of stainless steel AISI 304, reducing simulation runtime by 75%.

DEFORM Q-Form FEA Process Optimization

Formula Student E-Racing Car

Designed water cooling circuit for electric racing car using Siemens NX. Conducted thermal simulations and pressure loss simulations of cooling plates in Star CCM+ to optimize thermal management system performance.

Siemens NX Star CCM+ Thermal Analysis Racing
